This is a reality,
From which we cannot escape,
A dream that is not a dream,
A shadow over our heads,
That obscures our vision.

We emerged from soil,
Fertile and pure,
Untainted by delusions of intangibility,
A simple existence free from arrogance,
And from the tumors of possibility that stem from our hubris.

Fleshborne and free,
To explore and see,
Yet the boundaries decree,
That we are fleshbound and meek,
When truth lies beyond all constraints.

To you I ask,
When did we stop going by the old adage?
Of “which shall live and which shall die”,
To total submission and yearning for
“Fantasies of unlimited power and disembodied immortality”

A cure to one and many,
May be an unstoppable weapon beyond what we can see.
Sheltered and stable as the world is,
We choose to ignore the inevitability,
Of the exploitation of another’s salvation

A singularity,
A red line of inescapable attraction,
From the physical bodies from which we sought ascendency,
We may never return,
“Uncontrollable, irreversible, unfathomable”,
A growing horror seizes all functions,
While the technology we preached,
Remains impartial and unconcerned.
